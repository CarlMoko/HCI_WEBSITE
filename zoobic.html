About

Zoobic Safari is a 25-hectare safari park located in Subic Bay Freeport Zone in the Philippines.
It was established in 2004 as a part of the Subic Bay Metropolitan Authority's efforts to promote tourism in the area.
The park features a wide variety of animals, including tigers, lions, and crocodiles, as well as interactive attractions
such as a walk-through aviary and a zoocobia fun zoo.
Over the years, Zoobic Safari has become one of the top tourist attractions in Subic Bay, drawing visitors from all over the
country and abroad. The park is known for its commitment to animal conservation and education, and offers a range of programs
and activities aimed at raising awareness about wildlife preservation.
Visitors to Zoobic Safari can enjoy guided tours, animal encounters, and other immersive experiences that allow them to get
up close and personal with the park's residents. The park also hosts regular events and special exhibits, making it a popular
destination for families, school groups, and nature enthusiasts.
With its diverse array of animals, engaging attractions, and commitment to conservation, Zoobic Safari continues to be a
leading destination for wildlife lovers in the Philippines.
